# Sample Biology Notes - Cell Division

## Introduction to Cell Division

Cell division is the process by which a parent cell divides into two or more daughter cells. It is a fundamental process in all living organisms.

### Types of Cell Division

1. **Mitosis** - Results in two identical daughter cells with the same number of chromosomes as the parent cell. Used for growth and repair.

2. **Meiosis** - Results in four genetically unique daughter cells with half the number of chromosomes. Used for sexual reproduction.

## The Cell Cycle

The cell cycle consists of several phases:

- **G1 Phase**: Cell growth and normal function
- **S Phase**: DNA replication
- **G2 Phase**: Preparation for mitosis
- **M Phase**: Mitosis and cytokinesis

## Mitosis Stages

Mitosis proceeds through four distinct stages:

1. **Prophase**: Chromosomes condense and become visible. The nuclear envelope begins to break down.

2. **Metaphase**: Chromosomes align at the middle (metaphase plate) of the cell.

3. **Anaphase**: Sister chromatids separate and move to opposite poles of the cell.

4. **Telophase**: Nuclear envelopes reform around the separated chromosomes.

## Key Concepts

- **Chromosomes**: Structures containing DNA and proteins that carry genetic information
- **Chromatids**: Each half of a duplicated chromosome
- **Centromere**: The point where two sister chromatids are joined
- **Spindle fibers**: Protein structures that pull chromosomes apart during cell division

## Importance of Cell Division

Cell division is essential for:
- Growth of multicellular organisms
- Repair of damaged tissues
- Asexual reproduction in some organisms
- Production of gametes for sexual reproduction

## Cancer and Cell Division

Cancer is characterized by uncontrolled cell division. This occurs when:
- Cell cycle checkpoints fail
- DNA damage is not repaired
- Cells ignore signals to stop dividing
- Cells gain the ability to invade other tissues
